Slide 2 — The Problem (advance slide, then speak)

The Solution & The 4 Mini-Games
Slides 3 – 4

Slide 3 — The Solution (advance slide, then speak)
GrammarX solves this with gamification. The teacher creates a room, students join from their phone or computer, and everyone competes live — with instant feedback and a speed bonus on every answer.
Slide 4 — The 4 Mini-Games (advance slide, then speak)
There are four game modes: Time Machine for Past Simple, Duel Mode for Comparatives, Evidence File for Present Perfect, and Crystal Ball for the Future Tense.
💡 Tips for Luis Rodrigo When naming the four games, pause slightly between each one so the audience can follow.
Point to each card on the screen as you say its name.
Say "gamification" as: GAM-i-fi-CAY-shun.
Say "Comparatives" as: com-PAR-a-tivs.

How to Play
Slide 5

Slide 5 — How to Play (advance slide, then speak)
Using GrammarX is very simple. The teacher creates a room and shares a QR code. Students join from any device — no registration needed. Then everyone answers ten rounds of questions, and the fastest and most accurate player wins.
💡 Tips for Carla This is the most straightforward part — speak with confidence, you've got this.
Emphasize "any device" and "no registration" to highlight how accessible it is.
Say "QR code" as: CUE-ar code.
End on "wins" with a slight smile — it's a fun moment.

Slide 6 — Architecture & Tech Stack (advance slide, then speak)
We built it with React, Node.js, Socket.io, and SQLite — all communicating in real time through WebSockets. It is deployed live on Render Cloud.

For the URL on slide 7, say: "grammarx DOT onrender DOT com" — spell it out clearly.
